01
Data & Privacy

On your device. All case data – addresses, owner information, witness statements, scene photos, breaker panels, evidence logs, and exported PDFs – is stored locally on your iPad or Mac. It never leaves your device unless you explicitly share or export it yourself.

If you have iCloud enabled, your cases sync across your own Apple devices through your personal iCloud account. DocuFire never has access to that data.

No. We have no server, no database, and no backend that receives your case data. There is no mechanism by which anyone at DocuFire LLC can view, access, or retrieve your investigation files.

The only data that leaves your device is anonymous, non-identifiable diagnostic information collected by TelemetryDeck – crash reports and general performance metrics like app launch time. This data contains no names, no case details, and cannot be linked to any individual.

We do not collect contact info, location data, photos, financial information, or any case content.

02
AI Features

The AI assistant reviews your completed case documentation and can provide cause determination review, flag inconsistencies, suggest follow-up interview questions, and reference applicable NFPA 921 sections. It is a review tool – it does not make origin and cause determinations on your behalf.

The AI feature is optional and disabled by default.

The AI only sees structural and investigative content – fire patterns, cause documentation, utility information, and NFPA 921 compliance data.

Billing & Subscription

04
General

Yes. DocuFire is fully functional without an internet connection. You can create cases, document scenes, fill out all sections, and export PDFs entirely offline. The only features that require internet are the historical weather pull and the AI assistant.

iPad – All iPad models running iPadOS 16 or later. Optimized for iPad Pro with Apple Pencil support.

Mac – All Macs running macOS 13 Ventura or later via Mac Catalyst.

iPhone and Android are not currently supported. Android is on the roadmap.

Yes. DocuFire uses iCloud to automatically sync cases across all your Apple devices signed in to the same Apple ID. Start a case on your iPad at the scene and it will be available on your Mac when you get back to the office – no manual export or transfer needed.

From inside any case, tap the Export tab. Toggle which sections you want included – incident info, weather, evidence log, timeline, notes, checklist, and photo appendix are all individually selectable. Tap Export Case PDF and the standard iOS share sheet will appear, letting you AirDrop, email, save to Files, or send the report anywhere.
